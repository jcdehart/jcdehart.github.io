OLYMPEX 2015: I was a lead forecaster for the NASA OLYMPEX campaign, which aimed to better understand orographic modification of precipitation processes around Washington State's Olympic Peninsula and served as a ground validation for NASA's GPM satellite. Along with other members of the forecasting team, we led the daily forecasting briefings and assisted with realtime weather monitoring during aircraft missions.

HS3 2012-2014: I was a forecaster for the NASA HS3 campaign, which used unmanned Global Hawks to observe hurricanes and the tropical environment to understand processes affecting storm intensity. The forecasting team was responsible for providing daily forecasting briefings and realtime weather monitoring during aircraft missions.

VORTEX2 2010: A group of undergraduates at the University of Michigan supported the research team at Texas Tech University during VORTEX2 with tasks including locating appropriate sites for their mobile Ka-band Doppler radars and deploying StickNet meteorological observing stations.

SARP 2009: The Student Airborne Research Program (SARP) was an internship designed for undergraduates to get hands-on experience with field campaigns. As part of the Whole Air Sampler group, we collected in situ observations of trace gases, which were analyzed at the Rowland-Blake lab at UC Irvine.

MOBILE 2008: A group of undergraduates at the University of Michigan supported the research team at Texas Tech University with deployment of StickNet meteorological observing stations and Mobile Mesonet data collection.
